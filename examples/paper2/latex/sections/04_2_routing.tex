\subsection{Confidence-Aware Routing}
\label{sec:routing}

Given edge quality score $Q_{\mathrm{img}}$ and retry counter $r$, the router implements a testable three-way policy with threshold $\tau$ and budget $K$:
\begin{equation}
\text{route}(s)=
\begin{cases}
\text{upload\_cloud}, & Q_{\mathrm{img}}\geq\tau \land r\leq K,\\
\text{resample\_edge}, & Q_{\mathrm{img}}<\tau \land r\leq K,\\
\text{fail\_safe}, & r>K.
\end{cases}
\label{eq:routing}
\end{equation}

The retry budget $K$ limits the number of edge re-shoots before the system aborts; $K=2$ in our experiments.

\subsubsection{State machine nodes and edges.}
Table~\ref{tab:langgraph-nodes} enumerates the six nodes and their conditional edges implemented in \texttt{graph.py}. \texttt{TrialState} (a LangGraph-compatible TypedDict) carries \texttt{image\_path}, \texttt{q\_img}, \texttt{flags}, \texttt{retry\_count}, \texttt{route\_decision}, three ISO-8601 timestamps, and \texttt{latency\_ms}. All three branches and the $r>K$ guard are covered by unit tests (\texttt{tests/test\_routing.py}, 4 passing).

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{Confidence-aware routing loop (B2)}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\STATE $r \leftarrow 0$, capture frame $I$
\REPEAT
  \STATE $(Q,\texttt{flags}) \leftarrow \text{EdgeIQA}(I)$
  \STATE $d \leftarrow \text{Route}(Q,r,\tau,K)$ \COMMENT{see Eq. \ref{eq:routing}}
  \IF{$d = \texttt{upload\_cloud}$}
    \STATE \textbf{break} \COMMENT{quality sufficient, cloud upload}
  \ELSIF{$d = \texttt{resample\_edge}$}
    \STATE $r \leftarrow r+1$; recapture $I$
  \ELSE
    \STATE \textbf{fail\_safe}; \textbf{break} \COMMENT{$r>K$, abort trial}
  \ENDIF
\UNTIL{$r > K$}
\end{algorithmic}
\label{alg:routing-loop}
\end{algorithm}

On ShezhenV3 replay, each \texttt{resample\_edge} applies a \emph{physical skill transform} before re-scoring: \texttt{adaptive\_visual\_damping} reduces Gaussian blur radius on the clear reference frame; \texttt{exposure\_loop\_tuning} applies gain/bias correction when exposure flags fire (\S\ref{sec:implementation}, \texttt{resample\_physics.yaml}).
No stochastic score inflation is applied.

\subsubsection{Why LangGraph over a plain Python FSM?}
We benchmarked six alternatives on 1{,}000 trials (Table~S1, supplementary): LangGraph adds $\approx$3.6\,\textmu s/trial (0.004\% of a 360\,ms RTT budget), fully absorbed by the 10\,ms IQA step.
Beyond throughput, LangGraph provides checkpoint persistence, conditional-edge visualization (Fig.~\ref{fig:routing-flow}), and reuse in multi-agent orchestration (Paper~II).

\subsubsection{Algorithm extensions.}
Baseline B2 uses fixed $\tau_{B2}$ from validation.
Three extensions---dynamic $\tau_t$ (B2d), multi-frame EMA (B2m), and utility routing (B2u)---raise M2 from 0.60 to 0.65 on a quick ablation (60 frames, seed~0); full 3-seed results and equations are in the supplementary (Table~S2).

\subsection{Closed-Loop Protocol and Logging}
\label{sec:closed-loop}

Each trial executes \texttt{capture} $\rightarrow$ \texttt{edge\_iqa} $\rightarrow$ \texttt{route} $\rightarrow$ action. Wall-clock segment timestamps and the decision are appended as one JSON object per line (JSONL) for supplementary audit trails and auxiliary M6 trend analysis.

Required JSONL fields: \texttt{trial\_id}, \texttt{image\_path} (relative, privacy-safe), \texttt{q\_img}, \texttt{flags}, \texttt{retry\_count}, \texttt{route\_decision}, \texttt{t\_capture}, \texttt{t\_iqa}, \texttt{t\_route}, \texttt{latency\_ms}. Automated validation: \texttt{scripts/validate\_jsonl.py}.

The driver \texttt{python\ -m\ langgraph\_router.run\ --trials\ 10} produces \texttt{experiments/logs/run\_001.jsonl}. Main quantitative RTT (M1) is computed offline by \texttt{run\_matrix.py} on ShezhenV3 frames; JSONL logs serve protocol demonstration and trend verification only.

JSONL stores relative paths; supplementary samples use synthetic or de-identified frames. No raw patient data appears in Paper~I outputs.
