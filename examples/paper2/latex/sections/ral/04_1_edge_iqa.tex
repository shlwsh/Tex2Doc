\subsection{Edge Image Quality Assessment}
\label{sec:edge-iqa}

Edge-IQA scores each frame on CPU with $O(HW)$ cost on a $512\times512$ resize.
Let $S=\mathrm{Var}(\nabla^2 I)$ normalized to $[0,1]$ and $E$ an exposure term peaked at mid-gray:
\begin{equation}
Q_{\mathrm{img}} = 0.65\, S + 0.35\, E .
\end{equation}
Binary flags mark \texttt{blur} ($S<\tau_s$), \texttt{underexposed}, or \texttt{overexposed} means.
Robotic tongue capture fails mainly from motion blur and exposure drift; a single threshold $\tau$ gates LangGraph routing.
On ShezhenV3 validation with COCO tongue ROI, clear/blur medians are $0.516$ and $0.414$ ($\tau_{B2}{=}0.465$); CPU p95 is 9.6\,ms (\texttt{edge\_iqa/scorer.py}).
The implementation uses OpenCV-style image operators; the gateway spawns \texttt{python -m edge\_iqa.cli --json} in a subprocess to avoid GIL contention with \texttt{rclpy}.
Optional baseline B3 fine-tunes MobileNetV3-Small~\cite{howard2019mobilenetv3} on the train split; the primary deployable path is interpretable B2.

\begin{algorithm}[!t]
\caption{Edge-IQA scoring}
\label{alg:edge-iqa}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\STATE Resize $I$ to $512\times512$, convert to grayscale
\STATE $S \leftarrow \mathrm{normalize}(\mathrm{Var}(\nabla^2 I))$
\STATE $E \leftarrow 1 - | \mathrm{mean}(I) - 0.5 | / 0.5$
\STATE $Q_{\mathrm{img}} \leftarrow 0.65 S + 0.35 E$; derive flags from $(S,E)$
\STATE \RETURN $Q_{\mathrm{img}}$, flags
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}
