\subsection{Confidence-Aware Routing}
\label{sec:routing}

Given score $Q_{\mathrm{img}}$ and retry counter $r$, the router implements:
\begin{equation}
\text{route}(s)=
\begin{cases}
\text{upload\_cloud}, & Q_{\mathrm{img}}\geq\tau \land r\leq K,\\
\text{resample\_edge}, & Q_{\mathrm{img}}<\tau \land r\leq K,\\
\text{fail\_safe}, & r>K.
\end{cases}
\label{eq:routing}
\end{equation}
with $K{=}2$ in our experiments.
LangGraph nodes \texttt{capture} $\rightarrow$ \texttt{edge\_iqa} $\rightarrow$ \texttt{route} branch to resample, upload, or abort; \texttt{TrialState} carries \texttt{q\_img}, \texttt{flags}, \texttt{retry\_count}, and ISO-8601 timestamps.
Routing overhead ($\approx$3.6\,\textmu s/trial on 1{,}000 trials) is negligible versus the 9\,ms IQA step; LangGraph additionally provides checkpoint persistence and graph visualization (Fig.~\ref{fig:routing-flow}).

Each \texttt{resample\_edge} applies \emph{physical skill transforms} before re-scoring: \texttt{adaptive\_visual\_damping} reduces Gaussian blur radius on the clear reference; \texttt{exposure\_loop\_tuning} corrects gain/bias when exposure flags fire---no stochastic score inflation.
JSONL logs ten fields per frame for audit (\texttt{trial\_id}, \texttt{q\_img}, \texttt{flags}, \texttt{route\_decision}, timestamps, \texttt{latency\_ms}).

\begin{algorithm}[!t]
\caption{Confidence-aware routing loop (B2)}
\label{alg:routing-loop}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\STATE $r \leftarrow 0$; capture frame $I$
\REPEAT
  \STATE $(Q,\texttt{flags}) \leftarrow \text{EdgeIQA}(I)$
  \STATE $d \leftarrow \text{Route}(Q,r,\tau,K)$ \COMMENT{Eq.~\ref{eq:routing}}
  \IF{$d = \texttt{upload\_cloud}$} \STATE \textbf{break}
  \ELSIF{$d = \texttt{resample\_edge}$} \STATE $r \leftarrow r+1$; recapture $I$
  \ELSE \STATE \textbf{fail\_safe}; \textbf{break}
  \ENDIF
\UNTIL{$r > K$}
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}
